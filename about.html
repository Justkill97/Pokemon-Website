History of Pokemon

The history of Pokemon began in 1982, when a Japanese man named Satoshi Tajiri started a gaming magazine, Game Freak, with his friends, but he decided to start making his own video games instead of writing about them. Working with illustrator Ken Sugimori, he created the first Pokemon video game, Pokemon Red and Green, which was released in 1996. The game was innovative and captures audiences with its charming creatures.

The games sold millions of copies in the 1990s, and the name was shortened from Pocket Monsters to Pokémon. Since then, the franchise has expanded to TV shows, cards, movies, and merchandise, becoming a global phenomenon.

Favorite Pokemon

Ivysaur

The grass/poison-type pokemon, evolving from Bulbasaur. It has a large plant bulb on its back that absorbs sunlight for energy and attacks with its sharp leaves.

Wartortle

A water-type pokemon and it is the evolved form of Squirtle. It has exceptional swimming abilities and can shoot powerful jets of water from its mouth during battles.

Butterfree

A bug/flying-type pokemon, evolving from Metapod. Its wings have stunning patterns made by the release of powdery scales and is usually found fluttering among flowers.

Charmeleon

The fire-type pokemon and the evolved form of Charmander. It's characterized by its fiery temper and a flame burning at the tip of its tail, which gets hotter and brighter as it becomes stronger.
